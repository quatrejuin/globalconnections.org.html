Outcomes following Global Connections I

The appointment of a committee to be named "Zwelibanzi" to continue and expand the dialogue initiated during the Seminar between St. Stithians College (College, Collegiate, Prep & Junior School), the Letsibogo Girls' High School (Soweto), Reiger Park Primary School (Boksburg), Khayelihle School (Bergvlei), Pholosho School (Vosloorus) and Thutolore School (Soweto). The following areas were identified by the Committee as areas of concern:

  1. the need to network with our communities,
  2. develop fund-raising ideas,
  3. develop structures of "discipline without tears",
  4. hold a seminar on management and administration skills,
  5. teach children to lead in this new society of transformation,
  6. teacher development, and
  7. the sharing of resources.

The undertaking is to hold a seminar or conference at a neutral venue on each of the above topics. Specialist speakers will be brought in; a lot of group work will be done; networks will be built after each conference; and follow-up sessions will be held at each school.

The initiation of a Management Training workshop at St. Stithians College to further develop the collegial cooperation between independent school leaders and less-advantaged area school principals and to assist in the development of operational and management skills of the principals while sharing information on effective avenues for cooperation and expertise developed through experience in other countries and settings.

Placement of a Maru-a-Pula student from Botswana at Deerfield Academy, Massachusetts for the 1997-98 year on scholarship. During a visit by the Deerfield basketball coach and a student player to Maru-a-Pula in July, 1998, the two conducted a basketball clinic. The Chair of the Science Department at Deerfield to visit Maru-a-Pula and take part in one of MAP's week-long courses at its environmental camp on the Limpopo River in eastern Botswana. The Maru-a-Pula Director of O Level studies visited Deerfield in the Spring of 1998.

Two Students from Letsibogo Girls' School, Soweto, South Africa have received two year full residential scholarships at SCECGS/Redlands, Australia.

Starehe Boys' Centre and School, Kenya, received Grade 12 Director and two Housemasters from St. Stithians College, South Africa for a five day visit in March, 1998, resulting in Starehe's Baraza system of student meetings being introduced at St. Stithians under the name "Indaba". St. Stithians appointed a Community Service Officer with the intention of developing some such training as its Voluntary Service scheme. Subsequently, the Headmaster of St. Stithians, accompanied by his Sixth Form Master and five boys visited Starehe 11 - 16, June 1999.

The Hill School, Pennsylvania, hosted an eleventh grade Maru-a-Pula, Botswana student, in the fall of 1998. In the summer of 1998, three Hill students visited Maru-a-Pula's Bush Camp alongside the Limpopo River, studying vegetation and animal life. The students also spent time at Maru-a-Pula's main campus and safari camps in Cape Town.

Following GC I, an invitation was extended to Fred Okono, Starehe Boys' Centre and School, Kenya, to speak at the 51st General Meeting of the Conference of Heads of Independent Schools in South Africa, 7 -11 September, 1997, in Port Elizabeth. Outcomes following Global Connections XIII

My name is Jiang Xueqin, and I am director of the Peking University High School International Division in Beijing, China. In July 2011 the Global Connections community was kind enough to invite me to visit South Africa and Botswana with three dozen of the world's most passionate educators. The trip exposed me to the beauty of southern Africa and the warmth of its people, and permitted me to see the education possibilities and power of cross-cultural communication. During the trip, I quickly reached a student exchange agreement with Andy Taylor, Global Connections stalwart and the headmaster of Maru-a-Pula (MaP), which had hosted us in Gaberone.

This February we sent fifteen students and two teachers to MaP for cross-cultural learning and service learning. MaP students' love of learning and passion for life amazed our students, and the MaP community welcomed our students with open arms from the first day. In the service learning projects, while at first our students were afraid to interact with disabled children and AIDS orphans, they learned to overcome their fears and
prejudices, and to open their hearts and minds.

After the trip, we conducted an assessment of the educational impact of the trip. All fifteen students felt that service learning was the most valuable component of the trip, that helping others made them feel happy and fulfilled, and that they would like to continue service learning in China. We felt that the students had matured a great deal, and were eloquent when discussing the intellectual significance of the trip. In the classroom, we teachers have noticed that the students who went on the Botswana trip had become more curious, more motivated, and more creative.

The MaP community has inspired us to develop an intensive service learning program. We are planning a one-week trip in June to teach rural children in Guangxi, China. We also want to increase our students' geo-political awareness; we are currently devising a one-week trip to visit Israel to learn about their entrepreneurial culture, their prestigious engineering schools and research centers, the Holocaust, and the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

We at Peking University High School International Division have benefited greatly from our relationship with Global Connections. Besides Maru-a-Pula we also have a close relationship with Hotchkiss, which our students visit every summer to study literature and theater for three weeks. We hope to establish more student exchange programs with Global Connections schools soon. This July our headmaster Wang Zheng and his deputy Donald Holder will attend Global Connections in New Zealand to further develop Peking University High School's global network.

Our students' visit to Maru-a-Pula has transformed their lives, and forever changed the way they see themselves and the world. This would not have been possible without the Global Connections community, and we hope by sharing our story we'll encourage others to share their stories as well.
